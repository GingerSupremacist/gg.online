XBOX GAME REVIEWS

 

FIFA 20

PC | Xbox | PlayStation

 

Volta is a significant addition to FIFA and while it doesn't hit all the right notes, it's a largelt enjoyable way to play a wealth of content, including a story mode that's more concise and engaging than The Journey. Elsewhere, improvements have been made to the core FIFA 20 experience, but overall controlling the ball feels less fluid. For someone not attracted by the allure of Volta coupled with a neglet of offline modes such as Career, make it FIFA 20 an adequate but underwhelming entry into the series.

 

FORZA HORIZON 4

PC | Xbox

 

I'll always have a massive soft spot for the down under delight of FH3, but open-world racing has never looked as good as it does in FH4. It combines a beautiful world that's really four hugely distinct maps in one with a constantly rewarding and self-renewing racing experience and I really can't tear myself away from it. Playground Games hasn't just upped the ante once again; it's blown the bloody doors off.

 

COD: BLACK OPS 4

PC | Xbox | PlayStation

 

Each aspect of BO4 introduces some great ideas that evolve the series. Active healing in multiplayer gives players more control and makes for a great tactical experience. Heist mode is a wonderful palate cleanser. Zombies thrives from having more launch maps and the beginner mode is a fantastic way to ease into the action; Blackout is the most exciting of the three. When you're not experiencing technical issues, BO4 makes for an enjoyable shooter experience.

 

FRIDAY THE 13TH: THE GAME

PC | Xbox | PlayStation | Switch

 

As Jason, this is the best Friday the 13th game ever made. Hunting down players and executing them in gory scenes is an homage that warms my Friday-fan heart. But the fun of its multiplayer-only action is heavily skewed toward Jason - you'll mostly be stuck playing as teens rummaging through drawers. Despite lopsided gameplay and some glitches, there is some dumb fun to be had – which is all the movies ever really offered.
